Cheetah

Known for their jaw-dropping speed, striking coats, and unique social behaviours, these cats are full of surprises, but behind their beauty lies a species racing against extinction.

Scientific Name:
Acinonyx jubatus
Average Length:
1.1–1.5 meters (body), plus 60–80 cm tail
Average Lifespan:
10–12 years (wild)
Habitat:
Grasslands, savannas, and open plains of Africa (small population in Iran)

Tan with distinctive black spots and dark “tear marks” running from the eyes to the mouth, cheetahs are built for speed rather than strength. Their slender bodies, deep chests, and long tails help them balance while sprinting and making sharp turns. Unlike other big cats, cheetahs rely on explosive acceleration to chase down prey such as gazelles in short, high-speed bursts across open terrain.
